Senior UX Case Study

Mend: Humanizing AI Photo Restoration for Seniors

Mend is a mobile application designed to bridge the "tech-gap" for older users, transforming complex AI restoration into a dignified, accessible experience. Leveraging 11.5 years of industry experience, I developed this project to prove that high-level AI can be made intuitive for users with varying physical and cognitive needs.

I. Strategic Framing: The Problem

The "Tech-Gap" in AI

While Generative AI is rapidly evolving, seniors—the primary keepers of family heritage—are often excluded. Traditional restoration tools are clinical, complex, and rely on sliders that require high dexterity.

II. Discovery: User Journey

Phase Old Process Pain Point Emotional State Mend Solution
1. Scanning Glaring and blurry physical photos. Frustrated AI-Guided Scanning: Real-time feedback ensures a high-quality capture.
2. Restoration Complex sliders and technical jargon. Confused Conversational AI: Users type or speak simple requests like "Make eyes sharper".
3. Finalizing "Black box" AI processing feels opaque. Anxious System Transparency: Empathetic updates like "Mending Your Memory...".

III. Design & Implementation

Key Design Solutions & Accessibility

  • Inclusive Accessibility: Implemented 48px+ touch targets and high-contrast #C98D32 buttons for users with limited dexterity.
  • Heritage-First UI: Used a warm #F5F2E7 cream palette to evoke nostalgia and reduce digital eye strain.
  • LLM Integration: Replaced complex UI controls with a natural language "Refine with AI" interface.
